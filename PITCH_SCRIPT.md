# MicroDC Hub — 4-Minute Pitch Script & Storyline

Total runtime: **4:00**. Two acts: **Problem + Solution (0:00–2:30)**, **Live demo (2:30–4:00)**.
Tone: energy expert meets builder. One big idea: *the data center stops being a problem for the grid and becomes a partner in it.*

**Stack narrative:** NVIDIA + SPAN = hardware proof · MicroDC Hub (SiteScore + EarnMax) = software layer that scales deployment and earnings.

Format key: **[VISUAL]** = what's on screen · **VO** = spoken narration · timing is cumulative.

---

## ACT 1 — THE PROBLEM (0:00–0:50)

### 0:00–0:15 · Cold open — the wall

**[VISUAL]** Full-screen: a glowing AI data center at night, then a hard red "GRID CONNECTION DENIED — earliest slot 2031" stamp slams over it. Cut to a map of Europe with huge red "no-build" zones spreading.

**VO:**
> "Every new AI model needs a new data center. But the data center can't get built — not because of chips, but because of *power*. In half of Europe, the grid is full. The answer today is: wait five, seven years for a connection. That's the wall the whole industry just hit."

### 0:15–0:35 · Reframe — the wall is made of opportunity

**[VISUAL]** The red zones zoom in to reveal thousands of small green dots — rooftops, farms, workshops, swimming pools — each with spare solar, spare grid capacity, spare heat demand.

**VO:**
> "But here's what everyone's missing. While the *big* grid is congested, millions of small sites are sitting on surplus clean power they can't sell, and heat demand they pay to cover. The capacity isn't gone. It's just *distributed* — scattered across prosumers and small businesses who have no way to use it for compute."

### 0:35–0:50 · The three failures

**[VISUAL]** Three icons snap in: (1) a clogged power line, (2) a green leaf with a "value lost" tag, (3) two mismatched waveforms (solar peak vs. demand peak).

**VO:**
> "So we get three failures at once. Grid bottlenecks block new builds. Clean power and its certificate value go to waste. And rigid, always-on data centers can't flex to match when energy is cheap and green. Three problems — or, if you flip them, one opportunity."

## ACT 2 — THE BIG IDEA (0:50–1:35)

### 0:50–1:05 · The vision — democratize the data center

**[VISUAL]** A single big monolithic "hyperscale" block dissolves into a constellation of small glowing nodes across a city, each labeled with a postal code. Title fades in: **MicroDC Hub**.

**VO:**
> "Our idea is to *democratize* the data center. Instead of one giant facility waiting on one giant grid connection, picture a network of micro data centers — five kilowatts on a rooftop, fifty in a workshop — built exactly where the spare clean power already is."

### 1:05–1:20 · The hardware is already here — NVIDIA × SPAN

**[VISUAL]** Full-screen: the **NVIDIA In-Home Mini Data Center** diagram — a compact rack (16 GPUs, AMD EPYC, high-density RAM) fed by a **SPAN** smart power panel, powered by **unused household electricity** from the home, outputting **AI inference for home & community**. Hold 3–4 seconds, then a subtle overlay fades in: **"MicroDC Hub → where to deploy · how to earn"** linking the hardware to the platform.

**VO:**
> "This isn't science fiction. NVIDIA is already building the in-home mini data center — sixteen GPUs in a box the size of a fridge, powered by surplus electricity from your home, managed through SPAN's smart panel. The hardware layer exists. What's missing is the intelligence to *scale* it: where to put the next unit, and how to run it so the owner actually earns. That's MicroDC Hub."

### 1:20–1:35 · The society of shared compute

**[VISUAL]** `05-shared-society.png`: small node owners send compute up to a shared compute pool; **euros** flow back down. Two demand tiers at the top: **big tech / AI labs** and **micro-DC network operators** who aggregate and resell compute to companies and AI labs.

**VO:**
> "MicroDC Hub turns every prosumer site into a node in a shared compute economy. Households sell compute, not just power — and earn real revenue in euros. Medium operators can run whole networks of these units and serve AI labs directly. The energy factory of the last century made electricity. These new factories make compute — and we let everyone own a piece."

## Presentation ideas / production notes

- **Hardware proof beat (1:05):** use the real NVIDIA × SPAN diagram (`07-nvidia-span-hardware.png`) — credibility anchor before you pitch software. Judges need to believe the physical layer exists.
- **Stack slide logic:** Hardware (NVIDIA/SPAN) → Platform (MicroDC Hub) → Packages (SiteScore/EarnMax). Say it once in VO at 1:05, never repeat.
- **Visual motif:** one giant block → many small glowing nodes. Reuse at open, democratize beat, and close.
- **Color language:** keep the deck palette — near-black background, orange accent, green = good/earnings, red = constrained. The demo already matches, so the cut from slides to product feels seamless.
- **The "flip" beat (0:35):** this is the emotional hinge — same three facts, reframed from problem to opportunity. Slow down here; let it breathe.
- **No mock screenshots in Act 3:** the UI/UX changed, so we frame the two packages conceptually (one card) and prove them live in the demo. Don't show stylized mockups right before showing the real, slightly different product — the mismatch reads as sloppy.
- **Numbers discipline:** only two numbers before the demo (5–7 year wait, and the two-package framing). Save 82/100 and +€1,240 for the demo where they're shown live, not spoken.
- **Demo safety:** the demo is now ~2 minutes and carries both packages. Pre-record it at 1.0–1.2x with no dead air. Have the recording ready even if presenting live — never debug on stage.
- **Hand-off rhythm:** if two presenters, switch at 1:55 (visionary → builder) right as you cut to the live app. The voice change reinforces "vision, now proof."
- **One-sentence takeaway** for judges to remember: *"Rooftop solar let people sell power; MicroDC Hub lets them sell compute."*
- **Optional 10-sec sting** before the close if time allows: a single line on TAM — "Millions of prosumer sites in the EU. Every one is a potential node." Cut it first if you run long.
